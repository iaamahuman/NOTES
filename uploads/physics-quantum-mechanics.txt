Chapter 1: Wave-Particle Duality
---------------------------------

1. Historical Background
   - Max Planck's blackbody radiation (1900)
   - Einstein's photoelectric effect (1905)
   - de Broglie wavelength λ = h/p

2. Key Experiments
   - Double-slit experiment with electrons
   - Demonstrates wave-particle duality
   - Measurement affects the outcome (observer effect)

3. Wave Function ψ(x,t)
   - Complex-valued function describing quantum state
   - |ψ(x,t)|² gives probability density
   - Normalization condition: ∫|ψ|²dx = 1

Schrödinger Equation
-------------------

Time-dependent: iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ
Time-independent: Ĥψ = Eψ

Where:
- ℏ = h/2π (reduced Planck constant)
- Ĥ = Hamiltonian operator
- E = energy eigenvalue

Fundamental Principles
---------------------

1. Uncertainty Principle
   Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2
   - Position and momentum cannot be simultaneously measured with perfect precision

2. Superposition Principle
   - Quantum states can exist in superposition
   - ψ = c₁ψ₁ + c₂ψ₂ + ... + cₙψₙ
   - |c₁|² + |c₂|² + ... + |cₙ|² = 1

3. Measurement Postulate
   - Measurement collapses the wave function
   - Probability of measuring eigenvalue λᵢ is |cᵢ|²

Simple Systems
--------------

1. Particle in a Box
   - Infinite potential walls
   - Energy levels: Eₙ = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²)
   - Wave functions: ψₙ(x) = √(2/L) sin(nπx/L)

2. Harmonic Oscillator
   - V(x) = ½mω²x²
   - Energy levels: Eₙ = ℏω(n + ½)
   - Ground state: ψ₀(x) ∝ exp(-mωx²/2ℏ)
